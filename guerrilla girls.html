Guerrilla Girls

Print Files · Mobile App · Map · 2025

This project is inspired by the Guerrilla Girls and their use of bold typography and direct messaging to call out inequality in the art world. I focused on how type can carry both information and attitude, using scale, repetition, and placement to create a strong visual voice. I kept the palette simple with black and yellow to stay consistent with their visual language and to create high contrast. The layout plays with structure and disruption — some pages feel more rigid and grid-based, while others break that system to reflect tension and urgency in the message. I also explored how text can function as both content and image. By pushing type to fill space, rotate, and interact with the grid, the design becomes more expressive while still staying readable. The goal was to translate their activist tone into a contemporary layout, while experimenting with hierarchy, rhythm, and how information moves across a page.

Object Investigation Files

Poster Spreads

These spreads were created in InDesign as an exploration of layout and how typography can carry a message. Inspired by the Guerrilla Girls, I focused on using type as the main visual element rather than relying on imagery.

I experimented with scale, alignment, and hierarchy to see how different compositions could shift the tone and urgency of the message. Some layouts follow a structured grid, while others intentionally break it to feel more disruptive and expressive.

This process was about testing how far I could push type while still keeping the content readable, and understanding how layout decisions directly affect how the message is experienced.
